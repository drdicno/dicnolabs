Mortgage guide

How to Calculate Your Monthly Mortgage Payment

What is a mortgage payment?

A mortgage payment is the recurring amount a homeowner pays toward a home loan. The core payment usually includes principal, which reduces the loan balance, and interest, which is the cost of borrowing money. Many homeowners also pay property taxes and insurance through the monthly payment when those costs are held in escrow.

What does PITI mean?

PITI stands for principal, interest, taxes, and insurance. Principal and interest are tied directly to the loan. Taxes and insurance are housing costs that often change over time. A full mortgage estimate may also include PMI, HOA dues, and other recurring expenses.

Use the PITI Calculator to make these four categories easier to compare side by side.

The mortgage payment formula

For a fixed-rate loan, principal and interest are commonly estimated with this formula:

M = P x r(1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

In this formula, M is the monthly principal and interest payment, P is the loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the total number of monthly payments.

Worked example

Suppose a home costs $400,000 and the buyer makes an $80,000 down payment. The loan amount is $320,000. With a 30-year term and a 6.5% interest rate, the estimated principal and interest payment is about $2,023 per month. If yearly property taxes are $4,800 and yearly insurance is $1,200, the estimated total monthly payment becomes about $2,523 before optional PMI, HOA dues, lender fees, and closing costs.

Factors that affect monthly payments

The largest drivers of monthly payment are home price, down payment, loan term, and interest rate. Property tax rates, insurance premiums, PMI, HOA dues, and local rules can also change the total housing cost. If you are still exploring budget ranges, the Affordability Calculator can help connect monthly payment goals with home price ranges.

Tips for reducing mortgage costs

A larger down payment, lower interest rate, shorter shopping list of fees, or careful comparison between loan offers can reduce total borrowing costs. Refinancing may also help in some situations. Use the Refinance Calculator for comparing current and future loan scenarios.

Mortgage calculator FAQ

What does this mortgage calculator include?

It includes principal and interest, estimated monthly property tax, estimated monthly insurance, PMI, and HOA dues.

Is this a final loan quote?

No. This is an educational estimate. Actual payments can vary based on lender fees, escrow rules, taxes, insurance, and loan terms.

How is principal and interest calculated?

The calculator uses the standard fixed-rate amortization formula based on loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan term.

What is PMI?

PMI stands for private mortgage insurance. It may apply when the down payment is below a lender's required threshold.

Why are property taxes entered yearly?

Property taxes are often quoted annually. The calculator divides the yearly tax amount by 12 to estimate the monthly cost.

Why is home insurance entered yearly?

Home insurance premiums are commonly planned annually. The calculator converts the yearly amount into a monthly estimate.

Does the calculator include closing costs?

No. Closing costs, lender fees, title fees, prepaid items, and other one-time expenses are not included in the monthly payment estimate.

Can I compare different loan terms?

Yes. Change the loan term field to compare a 15-year, 20-year, 30-year, or other fixed-rate scenario.

How can I estimate affordability?

Start with a monthly payment you are comfortable with, then adjust the home price, down payment, rate, taxes, and insurance. The Affordability Calculator can help with that planning step.

When should I use a refinance calculator?

A refinance calculator is useful when comparing your current loan with a new rate, new term, fees, and estimated break-even timing.
